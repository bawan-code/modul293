The Author

Brandon Sanderson is a prolific and highly regarded American author of fantasy and science fiction, renowned for his intricate magic systems, expansive worldbuilding, and methodical, tightly structured storytelling.

Born in 1975 in Lincoln, Nebraska, Sanderson developed a love for fantasy literature early in life and later honed his craft at Brigham Young University, where he earned an M.F.A. in creative writing. He rose to prominence with the success of his Mistborn trilogy, a series celebrated for its unique magical mechanics and compelling characters.

Sanderson further solidified his place in the genre’s pantheon when he was chosen to complete The Wheel of Time series after the death of its original author, Robert Jordan—a monumental task that introduced his work to a broader audience and earned him critical acclaim. Beyond individual series, Sanderson is the architect of The Cosmere, a sprawling, interconnected universe in which many of his novels take place. This shared setting features subtle links and a hidden overarching narrative that rewards dedicated readers with deeper layers of meaning.

Known for his prodigious output and detailed worldbuilding, Sanderson has become one of the most influential figures in modern speculative fiction, continually pushing the boundaries of the genre.


The Story

The Cosmere is an ambitious and intricately designed shared universe created by Brandon Sanderson, spanning multiple series and stand-alone novels. These include Mistborn, The Stormlight Archive, Elantris, and Warbreaker, among others. Each story is set on a distinct planet, complete with its own unique cultures, histories, and meticulously crafted magic systems. While each series can be enjoyed independently, they are all subtly interconnected through a deeper, hidden narrative thread that unfolds across the larger Cosmere universe.

At the heart of this overarching mythos lies the concept of the Shards of Adonalsium—fragments of a divine power that were scattered across the cosmos, each influencing the worlds they inhabit in different ways. A number of recurring elements, such as the enigmatic world-hopper Hoid, mysterious organizations, and characters capable of traveling between planets, serve to link the stories together and hint at the Cosmere's greater scope.

Thematically, Sanderson’s Cosmere novels delve into profound questions surrounding divinity, power, identity, moral choice, and the cost of sacrifice. These philosophical undercurrents are seamlessly woven into narratives that feature intricate plots, vivid settings, and emotionally resonant character arcs. Sanderson’s masterful ability to blend these elements into a coherent and evolving universe has not only captivated readers but also helped redefine the possibilities of epic fantasy storytelling in the modern era.
